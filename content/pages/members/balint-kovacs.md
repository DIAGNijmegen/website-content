Bálint Kovács is a PhD student at the [Division of Medical Image Computing](https://www.dkfz.de/en/medical-image-computing/) at the [German Cancer Research Center (DKFZ)](https://www.dkfz.de/en/) and Medical Faculty Heidelberg, working on prostate cancer diagnosis on multi-parametric MRI and micro ultrasound, as well as on prostate cancer treatment via radiation therapy. He is also a visiting researcher at Radboud University Medical Center, working in the [Diagnostic Image Analysis Group (DIAG)](https://www.diagnijmegen.nl/). He received a double BSc degree in electronics and electrical engineering with embedded information and control systems specialization at the [Budapest University of Technology and Economics](https://www.bme.hu/en) and [Kyungpook National University](https://en.knu.ac.kr/main/main.htm) with the KEUDOS double-degree scholarship. He wrote his Bachelor's thesis on the development of optical biosensors. He was awarded a DAAD scholarship and continued his studies at the [Karlsruhe Institute of Technology](https://www.kit.edu/english/index.php), where he received his MSc degree in electrical engineering and information Technology with a specialization in Biomedical Engineering. In his Master’s thesis, he developed a brain-computer interface for the investigation of the human neuro-visual system using a deep learning approach.
